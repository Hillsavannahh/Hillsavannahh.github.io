Back Story

As a junior in highschool I can definitely understand the struggle of not being able to save your money even though we believe that since we were teenagers it doesn't matter. I started working when I was 12 and did many small hobbies like babysitting, cleaning my family's yards for money, etc. While being younger I didn't care where my money went, if I had seen something I liked I would've automatically bought it. I realized once I was actually old enough to get a real job that saving my money is very productive. My problem was that I believed since I was young there was no problem.

First Tip

One effective way of saving your money is to go to the ATM and take out half of what you got paid whether it's only 200 or 100. Use the other money you got paid to do whatever you want with it. I personally take my money out in cash and keep it in a secured spot in my house. This way can be hard because if you run out of your spending money it's typically really easy to convince yourself to just go grab a $20 and you'll be okay. You have to stay motivated in order to successfully reach your goal of saving money.

This is the main way I tend to save my own money based on the fact that I dont help with any bills or payments as of right now, my guardians deal with all of that, atleast until I become 18. This saving method is something I would only do when im younger just because of me not having to pay for anything bill wise.

40/40/20 or 50/30/20

This method is more for adults with jobs from college degrees and one of the easiest ways to keep your money seperated, 40% to taxes, save the other 40%, and use the 20% for your wants. Same way with 50/30/20, 50% to taxes, 30% to savings, and 20% again for your wants. I know a lot of people who use this method every paycheck and yes it may be a good idea I think it may be too organized for me.
